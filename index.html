About the Investment Club

The Investment Club is a student-led organization dedicated to understanding the stock market, analyzing current events, and building financial literacy through real-world simulations and bi-weekly discussions.

We meet every other Wednesday at 7:45 AM in Room 1423. Each meeting begins with an educational presentation to introduce and reinforce key investment concepts. We then examine current global events and their impact on financial markets—always maintaining a nonpartisan approach. Club members are encouraged to present their own trading ideas and potential stock picks to the group.

As a team, we create and manage a simulated portfolio using the Investopedia paper trading platform, tracking and analyzing our collective performance. Additional events include a fantasy football-style investing competition, where members build individual portfolios and compete to maximize returns. The member with the lowest balance by the deadline will receive a fun, group-decided punishment—voted on via Google Forms.

We use platforms such as Yahoo Finance for market research and Investopedia for simulations. While members may choose to invest real money through services like Fidelity Teen Accounts (with parental supervision), any personal financial decisions are the sole responsibility of the member.

Our mission is to educate and empower—not to provide financial advice. Officer roles and a point-based system for graduation cords are in development. The club welcomes feedback through anonymous Google Forms or in person.

For any questions, feel free to reach out to Jacob Kirkpatrick (Founder), Colin Wilbanks (Founder), or Mr. Erwin (Club Sponsor) using the information in the Contact Us section.

Our Goals

  1. Promote Financial Literacy: Help members develop a strong foundation in investing and personal finance through accessible education.
  2. Understand the Stock Market: Explore how the stock market functions, including concepts like risk, return, and portfolio diversification.
  3. Apply Knowledge Through Simulation: Use paper trading platforms like Investopedia to practice investment strategies in a risk-free environment.
  4. Analyze Current Events: Examine global and national news to understand how economic trends and events influence the market.
  5. Encourage Collaboration & Leadership: Offer opportunities for members to lead discussions, present stock ideas, and take on officer positions within the club.
